SAMOSA RECIPE

DESCRIPTION

A samosa is a popular, deep-fried South Asian pastry with a crisp, flaky crust

It is traditionally shaped into a triangle or cone and packed with a savory, spiced filling, most commonly made of mashed potatoes, peas, and aromatic spices

INGREDIENTS

  • All-purpose flour
  • Carom seeds
  • Ghee or oil
  • Water
  • salt
  • Boiled potatoes
  • Green peas
  • Whole spices
  • Spice powders
  • Fresh coriander leaves

STEPS

  1. Mix ingredients: Combine flour, carom seeds, salt, and melted ghee in a bowl.
  2. Mix ingredients: Combine flour, carom seeds, salt, and melted ghee in a bowl.
  3. Knead stiffly: Add ice-cold water gradually to knead a very firm, tight dough.
  4. Rest dough: Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for 30 minutes
  5. Saute spices: Heat oil in a pan and splutter cumin, coriander, and fennel seeds.
  6. Add aromatics: Stir in the ginger-green chilli paste and cook for one minute.
  7. Mix vegetables: Add the peas, mashed potatoes, spice powders, and salt to the pan
  8. Finish filling: Mash lightly, cook for 3 minutes, stir in fresh coriander, and let it cool completely.
  9. Roll sheets: Divide the dough into balls and roll each into an oval shape.
  10. Cut & cone: Cut the oval in half, brush water on the straight edge, and fold into a cone.
  11. Stuff & seal: Stuff the cone with filling, pinch the bottom edges tightly together to seal.
  12. Fry low & slow: Deep-fry in medium-low hot oil for 10–12 minutes until golden and crisp.
